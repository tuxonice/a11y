A Pastel de Nata is a traditional Portuguese custard tart that is beloved for its rich and creamy texture. It features a flaky, crispy pastry shell filled with a smooth, sweet custard made from egg yolks, sugar, cream, and a hint of vanilla. The top of the custard is often caramelized, giving it a slightly burnt, golden-brown appearance that adds a subtle, smoky flavor. These tarts are typically enjoyed warm, often sprinkled with a dusting of cinnamon or powdered sugar. Originating from the Jerónimos Monastery in Lisbon, Pastéis de Nata have become a quintessential part of Portuguese cuisine, celebrated for their balance of textures and flavors.
